Choosing where to study in the UK is rarely about a single league table. The right university is the one whose **course content, city, and community** match the life you want for the next three to four years.

## Start with the course, not the brand

A "lower ranked" university with a stronger department for your subject will almost always serve you better than a famous name with a mediocre programme. Read the module list. Look up the lecturers. Check whether the final-year projects sound like work you'd be proud of.

## Visit (or virtually visit) the city

You will spend more hours in the city than in lecture theatres. Ask yourself:

- Can you afford rent here on a realistic budget?
- Is there a community for your interests, faith, or culture?
- Will the commute drain you, or energise you?

## Graduate outcomes matter — but read them carefully

A high graduate salary often reflects the **subject** more than the university. Compare like-for-like courses, and weight industry links, placement years, and alumni networks.

## A simple shortlist rule

Aim for **one stretch, three solid matches, and one safe choice**. Your stretch should excite you. Your safe choice should still feel like a place you'd genuinely attend.
