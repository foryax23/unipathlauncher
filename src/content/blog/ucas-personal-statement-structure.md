The personal statement is the only place admissions tutors hear your voice. Use it to show **how you think**, not just what you've done.

## The four-paragraph spine

1. **Why this subject** — a specific moment, idea, or problem that drew you in. Not "from a young age".
2. **Academic evidence** — two or three examples of independent reading, projects, or coursework that prove the interest is real.
3. **Wider engagement** — competitions, work experience, MOOCs, or societies that show initiative.
4. **Beyond academics** — a short, honest paragraph on what you'll bring to campus life.

## Openings that land

- "The first time a regression line predicted something I cared about, I understood why economists trust data."
- "Three weeks shadowing a paediatric nurse rewrote what I thought medicine actually was."

## Openings to avoid

- "From a very young age, I have been fascinated by…"
- A famous quotation followed by no analysis.
- A list of every prize you've won.

## Edit ruthlessly

Read it out loud. If a sentence doesn't earn its place, cut it. Tutors read thousands — clarity is kindness.
